About Ray's

A quintessential Seattle dining destination, Ray's Boathouse is internationally renowned for its ultimate seafood experience and breathtaking waterfront view of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains. Savor sustainable seafood, local produce, organic meats, house made desserts, and Northwest-centric wines.

Our Story

What began as a dockside cafe known for its home-cooked meals has become a must-visit Seattle restaurant with a majestic view of Puget Sound and the rugged Olympic Mountains. our_story_1In 1939 the original owner, Ray Lichtenberger, moved his growing boat rental and bait house to the current location and in 1945 opened a coffee house. By 1952, he’d built the neon sign that flashes “RAY’S” in bold, red letters on the dock overlooking Shilshole Bay at the crossing point to Puget Sound and the Hiram M. Chittenden Locks leading to Lake Washington. Through the 1960’s Ray’s operated as both a casual fish-and-chips cafe and boat rental. In 1973 Russ Wohlers, Earl Lasher and Duke Moscrip bought Ray’s Boathouse and quickly refurbished the structure, transforming into a nationally respected seafood restaurant while maintaining its cordial, glad-to-see-you atmosphere. While Moscrip left to pursue other restaurant ventures, Elizabeth Gingrich joined the owner team in 1975 and former Seattle Sonic Jack Sikma joined in 1986. Under Wohlers guidance, Ray’s built its reputation on seasonal dishes prepared simply to highlight the flavors of impeccably fresh seafood and the freshest locally grown produce. Ray’s became part of what has been called a food revolution in the Pacific Northwest, helping to introduce a fashionable and distinctive regional cuisine built around Northwest products, microbrews and wines. our_story_2Ray’s was the first to reintroduce to Seattleites Olympia oysters, the region’s only native oyster, and heralded Northwest delicacies such as singing scallops, Loughborough Inlet spot prawns, Copper River Salmon, Bruce Gore “frozen at sea” salmon and the concept of red wine with fish. In 1976, Ray’s was also the first local restaurant to purchase its own wholesale fish buyer’s license, allowing it to buy directly from the fishermen, ensuring the freshest catch. On May 26, 1987, at the height of its popularity, Ray’s Boathouse burned to the pier. The four-alarm fire was reported by major newspapers across the country and footage of the fire appeared on national newscasts. Seattleites responded overwhelmingly to rebuild the landmark and a new Boathouse opened in April 1988. On May 23, 1997, Ray’s suffered its second fire nearly ten years to the day of the first fire. Fortunately, damage was contained to the Boathouse kitchen and the restaurant reopened in July 1997. our_story_3Ray’s maintains a strong commitment to the community by participating in local fundraisers and supporting local charities. Sustainability practices and nurturing relationships with local purveyors also remains a strong focus. In 2013, Ray’s celebrated its 40th birthday year with a variety of activities to thank the community that has supported Ray’s over the decades. With a complete overhaul of the Boathouse dining room in late 2012, under the direction of Eric Hentz and his team at Mallet, Inc., Rays is looking ahead to the next 40 years with passion and excitement.

Ray's Cafe

Ray’s Café will warm your soul with seasonal fresh takes on classic dishes, from our signature clam chowder to in-house smoked salmon, piping hot steamers and small plate wonders. Ray’s waterfront bar offers casual dining and relaxation in the fresh sea air. We have 20 microbrews on tap, extensive wines and our own specialty hand-crafted cocktail list. Ray’s Café offers succulent seafood with a side of warm hospitality for lunch and dinner and there’s no place like Ray’s Café bar for the best happy hour in Ballard every day… with a backdrop of the Puget Sound and Olympic Mountains. Did we mention our outdoor deck? It’s the answer to every Seattleite’s dream after a long, dark winter. Open seasonally, the Ray’s Café waterfront deck is the perfect place to soak up the splendor of the Pacific Northwest. Just bring your sunnies and let us take care of the rest! Ray’s Café is perfectly suited for fun and celebration throughout the year. Reservations are accepted and recommended, walk-ins are welcome. Parking is a cinch in our free private lot, along with complimentary valet parking during our busiest times.

Ray’s Boathouse

Ray’s gifted culinary experts demonstrate passion for every seared salmon, sake kasu marinated sablefish and braised lamb shank they prepare, pouring their hearts into every detail of the complex sauces and syrups. Inspired by our chefs’ endless creativity, Ray’s Boathouse menu will delight your senses, while our knowledgeable wine director offers an extensive wine list featuring local and international vintages. Ray’s Boathouse bar features both classic and specialty cocktails, as well as a selection of delectable bites from the Boathouse kitchen. You may want to culminate your culinary journey on a sweet note. Choose from an array of creative confections designed by our talented in-house pastry team. At Ray’s Boathouse, we pride ourselves on friendly, attentive service with the pleasant hospitality that marks the best restaurants in Seattle. Bring your friends, family and visiting guests to Ray’s Boathouse for an unforgettable culinary experience. Complimentary parking is offered in our private lot, in addition to complimentary valet service during our busiest times.
